How privacy works & limitations

What this page does

  • Reads your text aloud using text-to-speech voices already installed on your computer.
  • Keeps your draft in the page only. It is not saved anywhere by this page — closing or reloading the tab discards it.

What this page does not do

  • It does not upload your text to any server of mine.
  • It does not save your manuscript (only harmless settings like chosen voice, speed and theme are stored locally).
  • It does not use AI to process your writing.
  • It does not include analytics, tracking, external fonts, or third-party scripts.

Important limitation — voices

Voice privacy depends on your browser, operating system, and the voice you pick. Some browsers offer cloud/online voices that do send your text to a provider (e.g. Microsoft or Google) to be synthesised. This tool tries to identify local voices — labelling them where the browser exposes that information — and defaults to “Local voices only.” Where the browser doesn't tell us, treat a voice as unknown. For maximum privacy, use a known local system voice (on Windows these are often named Microsoft David, Zira, Mark, etc.) and run offline.

For maximum privacy

  • Click “Download this page for offline use.”
  • Disconnect from the internet.
  • Open the downloaded file in your browser.
  • Paste your draft and listen locally.

Disclaimer: This tool is designed for privacy-conscious writers. It runs locally in your browser and does not intentionally send your text anywhere, but no software can be guaranteed private in every environment. You should only use software from sources you trust. The whole tool is this one HTML file — you're encouraged to inspect it (View Source) before trusting it with a manuscript.

This page makes no intentional network requests. It uses only your browser's built-in speechSynthesis API. Open your browser's DevTools → Network tab and press Play to verify.
